Galway's land birds sanctuary

(Located 1 Km from the town) Laying a few kilo meters east of the main town is a montage forest called Galwayâ€™s land. This little patch of land is famous for its bird life, which includes 10 Sri Lankan endemics. It also offers the visitor to view wild boar, barking deer and other animals. For the more adventurous visitor this is an ideal place to visit as it can be reached only on a walking trail.

Things To Do

01.Birds watching

The park is known for its rich bird diversity, with around 30 endemic bird species and 20 rare migratory species living here.These species thrive in the dense undergrowth and montane forests of the park, making it a prime spot for birdwatching.

02.Photography and Picnicking

The park's lush landscapes, misty mornings, and vibrant flora provide excellent photography opportunities. Designated picnic areas allow visitors to relax and enjoy meals amidst nature. Please ensure to maintain cleanliness to preserve the park's pristine environment.
